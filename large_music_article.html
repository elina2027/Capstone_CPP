Introduction to Music

The Evolution of Musical Instruments

The story of musical instruments begins with the human voice, the most natural and versatile instrument available to our ancestors. The fox and other animals taught early humans about the variety of sounds possible in nature. From simple percussion instruments made from hollow logs to the sophisticated electronic synthesizers of today, the development of musical instruments reflects humanity's endless quest to expand our sonic possibilities. The discovery that stretched strings could produce different pitches led to the creation of early harps and lyres, while the observation that blown air could create musical tones resulted in the first flutes and whistles.

The Romantic era saw composers like Beethoven, Tchaikovsky, and Brahms pushing the boundaries of emotional expression in music.
